Comprehensive Earnings Impact Analysis
Observation: The four-panel analysis reveals weak correlation between pre-earnings and earnings day returns, a distribution of earnings day returns centered around zero, mixed 10-day window performance, and a low 37.5% success rate.

Interpretation: The lack of consistent positive earnings reactions suggests that the market efficiently incorporates NVIDIA's strong fundamentals well before earnings announcements. The negative average earnings day return (-0.50%) indicates potential "buy the rumor, sell the news" behavior.

Statistical Significance Testing

Our rigorous statistical analysis shows that both earnings day returns (p-value: 0.484) and 10-day window returns (p-value: 0.662) are not statistically significant at the 5% level. This means we cannot reject the null hypothesis that earnings returns average zero, confirming that earnings announcements do not provide reliable positive returns.

Key Insights and Conclusions

Myth Busted: Earnings-Driven Surge

The popular narrative that NVIDIA's spectacular 314% surge was driven by earnings announcements is demonstrably false. Our analysis of 8 earnings events over two years shows:

  • Average earnings day return: -0.50% (not statistically significant)
  • Only 1 out of 8 earnings days produced positive returns
  • 10-day earnings window success rate: 37.5% (worse than random)
  • Trading strategy return: -13.8% vs 314% buy & hold

Investment Implications

1. Avoid Earnings Timing: Attempting to trade around NVIDIA earnings is a losing strategy that underperforms buy & hold by over 300 percentage points.

2. Focus on Fundamentals: NVIDIA's gains come from sustained AI boom, data center growth, and technological leadership, not quarterly surprises.

3. Valuation Risk: Current P/E of 54x represents extreme optimism and leaves little margin for error.

4. Long-term Approach: Buy & hold has proven vastly superior to any earnings-based timing strategy.

Market Efficiency Insights

NVIDIA's case demonstrates classic market efficiency principles:

  • Information Incorporation: Market prices NVIDIA's AI leadership well before earnings
  • Random Walk: Earnings day returns show no predictable pattern
  • Arbitrage Elimination: Simple timing strategies fail due to efficient pricing
  • Behavioral Finance: "Buy the rumor, sell the news" behavior evident
